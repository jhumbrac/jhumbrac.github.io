so how do you do that?

First you must ask yourself: What does your site do?

What type of website are you building?

  • Blog?
  • E-commerce?
  • Business page?
  • Something else?
  • Some combination of the above?

What does a successful interaction with your users look like?

Do you want them to fill out your contact form? Call you on the phone? Read your blog? Buy something from you? In the marketing world this is known as a Conversion. It simply means that you got your users to do what you wanted them to do.

Many websites have multiple conversions too

  • User capture
  • A shop attached to your blog
  • A blog attached to your business
  • Newsletter signups
  • Social shares & follows

Content vs Style & What is the role of each?

What is Content?

In short this is your HTML, but more specifically web content is the textual, visual, or aural content that is encountered as part of the user experience on websites. It may include — among other things — text, images, sounds, videos, and animations.*

Thank you Wikipedia!

What is Style?

Style refers to how HTML elements are displayed on your page. When we talk about style we'll often be referring to the code in your CSS.

How do they interact?

HTML provides the building blocks upon which your entire page will be structured. CSS is then used to manipulate & shape the HTML. Your page design is the result of how your HTML elements interact with each other.

Think of the HTML as the skeletal structure of the page. The CSS is the skin.*

JavaScript would be the muscles in this analogy

Wasting time is expensive! So what's the easiest & most efficient way to build a website?

  1. Plan, plan, plan, & plan some more!

    Plan out every last detail of your page before you begin. In the same way a contractor wouldn't build a house without blueprints, a comprehensive content plan streamlines the process & increases efficiency.

  2. Code your HTML

    It's important to code the entirety of your HTML before working on your CSS. This means get all your text, images, icons, videos etc on the page.

  3. Style your page

    Make sure your style reflects and accentuates the content. Don't distract from your message or make everything hard to read.

Now it's time to plan your content

Every Website needs to immediately answer these questions

  1. Who are you, & what do you do?

    Branding 101 - your users want to ensure that you do the thing they're looking for. If this is not addressed ASAP your users will leave!

  2. Why should your users choose you?

    You must convince your users that your (insert conversion) is worth doing. If you don't your users will move on to your competition.

  3. How do they do what you want them to do?

    Make it easy for your users to convert. No one wants to solve logic puzzles before buying a t-shirt.

Pro tip: sketch out an outline like you used to in school to help you get your content together and ensure everything makes sense

Many of your users will be using disability assistance software to "view" your page. It's important to make sure your page can be understood without styling so as not to alienate these users. As of January 2018 websites have to be ADA* compliant

Americans with Disabilities Act

Once your content is finalized you can begin designing your page
